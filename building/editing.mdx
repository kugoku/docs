Your site doesn't stop being editable once it's published. You can return to the chat at any time — days, weeks, or months after you first built it — and ask Golem to make changes. Whether you want to update a phone number, add a new page, or completely rethink the color scheme, the process is the same: describe the change in chat and Golem applies it.

## Open the chat on an existing project

Go to your Goleme dashboard and open the project you want to edit. The chat with Golem is always available inside the project view. Type your change request and send it. Golem will update the site and show you the result.

You can make as many changes as you need in a single session, or come back later and pick up where you left off.

<Note>
  Each edit that triggers a new AI generation consumes credits from your account. Minor follow-up clarifications within the same session may be grouped, but plan for each substantive change request to use credits. See [AI credits](/billing/credits) for details.
</Note>

## Types of edits you can make

### Text changes

Update any written content on your site — headings, body text, button labels, footer details, and more.

> "Change the headline on the homepage to 'Handcrafted furniture for modern homes'."
> "Update the phone number in the footer to +44 7700 900123."
> "Rewrite the About page in a friendlier, more conversational tone."

### Color and style changes

Ask Golem to change the visual appearance of your site.

> "Change the primary color to deep navy blue."
> "Make the buttons rounded and use a white background throughout."
> "Switch to a dark mode color scheme."

### Adding and removing features

Ask Golem to add new functionality or remove sections you no longer need.

> "Add a newsletter signup form to the bottom of the homepage."
> "Remove the testimonials section from the Services page."
> "Add a cookie consent banner."

### New pages

You can add pages to an existing site at any time — see [Pages & content](/building/pages-and-content) for more examples.

> "Add a Careers page listing two open positions: a front-end developer and a customer support agent."

## Describe changes clearly

The clearer your instruction, the more accurate the result. Give Golem enough context to understand exactly what you want changed and how.

**Be specific about location and content:**

> "On the Contact page, change the form's submit button label from 'Send' to 'Request a callback'."

**Explain the goal, not just the action, when helpful:**

> "The Services page feels too long. Break it into two sections — one for residential clients and one for commercial clients — so visitors can jump to what's relevant."

## Iterate until it's right

If Golem's first attempt isn't exactly what you had in mind, follow up with a correction. You don't need to start over — just describe what needs adjusting.

> "That's close, but make the hero text larger and center it."
> "The new color is too dark. Try a lighter shade of the same green."
> "Good — now do the same thing on the About page."

<Tip>
  **Example edit prompts:**

  - "Change the site font to something more modern and clean — try a sans-serif."
  - "Add an FAQ section to the bottom of the Pricing page with three questions about billing."
  - "The homepage hero image placeholder takes up too much space on mobile. Make it smaller."
  - "Rename the 'Services' page to 'What we do' and update the navigation link to match."
  - "Add a WhatsApp chat button that opens a conversation with +44 7700 900456."
</Tip>
