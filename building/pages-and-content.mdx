Every Goleme site is made up of pages, and each page contains the content Golem generates based on your instructions. You don't manage pages through a visual editor — you describe what you want in the chat and Golem builds it. This applies both when you first create your site and any time you want to add or change pages and content later.

## How pages are structured

When Golem builds your site, it creates a set of pages based on your initial description or the template you chose. Each page has its own URL path (for example, `/about` or `/services`) and appears in your site's navigation automatically. Golem decides on a sensible structure, but you can always ask it to add, rename, or remove pages.

A project is a single website. If you need a completely separate site — with its own domain, pages, and content — create a new project.

## Ask Golem to add a new page

To add a page to your existing site, open the chat on your project and describe the page you want.

**Examples:**

- "Add a FAQ page with answers to the five most common questions about shipping and returns."
- "Create a Team page that introduces our three co-founders with a short bio and photo placeholder for each."
- "Add a blog section with a listing page and individual article pages."
- "Create a Pricing page with three tiers: Basic, Pro, and Enterprise."

Golem will generate the new page and add it to your navigation. You can then ask for further adjustments.

## Add specific content types

Golem can add a wide range of content to any page. Describe what you want and where it should appear.

### Text and headings

Ask Golem to add, rewrite, or restructure any text on your site. You can specify tone, length, and purpose.

> "Add a short introductory paragraph to the homepage that explains what we do in two sentences."

### Images and galleries

Golem can add image placeholders or gallery sections that you can populate with your own photos.

> "Add a photo gallery section to the Portfolio page with a grid layout."

### Pricing tables

> "Add a pricing table to the Pricing page with three columns. The plans are Starter at €9/month, Growth at €29/month, and Pro at €79/month."

### Contact forms

> "Add a contact form to the Contact page with fields for name, email, phone number, and a message. Include a submit button."

### Booking and scheduling sections

> "Add a booking section to the Services page with a button that links to our Calendly."

### Navigation and footer

> "Add a link to the new FAQ page in the main navigation. Add our address and business hours to the footer."

## Tips for describing content

Be specific about the content itself, not just the type. The more detail you give, the less you'll need to edit afterwards.

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---|---|
| "Add a services section" | "Add a services section with three cards: Web Design, SEO, and Branding. Each card should have a short two-sentence description." |
| "Add a contact form" | "Add a contact form with fields for name, email, and project budget. Label the submit button 'Get in touch'." |
| "Add pricing" | "Add a pricing section with two plans. Basic is free with 3 projects. Business is 1,199 CZK/year with 5 projects and a custom domain." |

If Golem's first attempt isn't quite right, follow up with a more specific instruction. You can correct, expand, or reshape any piece of content through the chat.
