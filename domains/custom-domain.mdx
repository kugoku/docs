If you own a domain name — whether you registered it with a Czech registrar, GoDaddy, Namecheap, or anywhere else — you can connect it to any Goleme project. Once connected, visitors who go to your domain will see your Goleme site. The connection works by updating a DNS record at your domain registrar to point traffic to Goleme's servers.

<Warning>
Connecting a custom domain requires a **Business** or **Agency** plan. The free Explorer plan only supports the automatically assigned `yoursite.goleme.ai` subdomain. [Compare plans](https://goleme.ai/pricing) to upgrade.
</Warning>

## Requirements

Before you start, make sure you have:

- A **Business** or **Agency** Goleme plan
- A domain name you own, registered with any domain registrar
- Access to the DNS settings for that domain (usually in your registrar's control panel)
- A published Goleme project to connect the domain to

## Connect your domain

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your project settings">
    Go to your [Goleme dashboard](https://goleme.ai) and open the project you want to connect a domain to. Navigate to the **Settings** tab within the project.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your domain">
    Find the **Custom domain** section and enter the domain name you want to use — for example, `www.yourbusiness.cz` or `yourbusiness.cz`. Click **Add domain** to continue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the DNS values">
    Goleme will display the DNS record you need to create. Copy the record type, name, and value shown on screen. You will enter these into your registrar's DNS settings in the next step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update your DNS records">
    Log in to the control panel of the registrar where your domain is registered. Open the DNS settings for your domain and create a new record using the values from the previous step:

    - For a **root domain** (e.g., `yourbusiness.cz`): create an **A record** pointing to the Goleme IP address shown.
    - For a **subdomain** (e.g., `www.yourbusiness.cz`): create a **CNAME record** pointing to the Goleme hostname shown.

    Save the record. The exact steps vary by registrar — consult your registrar's help documentation if you are unsure where to find DNS settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the connection">
    Return to your Goleme project settings. Goleme will automatically check for your DNS record. Once the record is detected, the domain status changes to **Connected**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish to your custom domain">
    After the domain is connected, publish your project. Your site will be live at your custom domain. See [Publishing](/domains/publishing) for the full publish flow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
DNS changes can take anywhere from a few minutes to 48 hours to propagate across the internet, depending on your registrar and the TTL (time-to-live) setting on your records. If your domain does not appear connected immediately, wait a while and check again.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

**The domain status stays "Pending" for a long time.** DNS propagation can take up to 48 hours. Double-check that you entered the correct record type and value. A common mistake is creating a CNAME for a root domain — use an A record for root domains instead.

**I see a browser warning after connecting.** SSL certificates are provisioned automatically after the domain is verified. This usually takes a few minutes once DNS propagates.

**I used up my custom domain allowance.** Each plan has a limit on the number of custom domains you can connect. Business plans allow 1 custom domain; Agency plans allow 5. Upgrade to a higher plan or contact Goleme to discuss a tailored plan if you need more.
